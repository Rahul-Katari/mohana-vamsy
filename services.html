Oncology | Cancer Treatment

Oncology is a cancer treatment that uses surgery to remove cancer cells from the body. This can involve cutting out the tumour and any nearby tissues that cancer might affect. The goal of oncology is to remove as much of the cancer as possible while also preserving the patient's overall health and quality of life. This type of treatment can be very complex and requires specialised training. Still, it can be an essential part of cancer care for many people.

Oncologist | Cancer Specialist

A Oncologist is a medical professional specialising in treating cancer. They work as a team of doctors to develop an individualised treatment plan for each patient.

Oncologist

Oncologists are responsible for removing tumours, comprehending how cancer operates, and staying updated with the latest research in the field. They collaborate with other cancer specialists to ensure that every patient receives the most effective treatment, including chemotherapy, radiation therapy, or other targeted therapies. The primary objective of a surgical oncologist is to aid cancer patients in improving their health, prolonging their life, and achieving the best possible quality of life.

The most popular questions to discuss about Health

Oncologists treat various cancers and associated illnesses.

Oncologists use a combination of methods to diagnose cancer accurately, including various approaches to identify the type and stage of cancer, which is crucial for determining the most effective treatment plan. The methods to diagnose cancer include:

  • Physical Examination
  • Laboratory Tests
  • Imaging tests
  • Biopsy
  • Endoscopic Examinations
  • Genetic tests

During your first visit, an oncologist will evaluate your medical history and symptoms and perform a physical exam. Additional tests may be recommended to confirm the diagnosis and determine the cancer's stage. The oncologist will explain the diagnosis, recommended tests, and next steps of the care plan. You can ask questions, discuss treatment options, or get referred to other specialists.

To treat cancer, oncologists have access to a variety of treatments; however, the efficacy and availability of these treatments differ according to the type of cancer, its stage, and other variables, such as the patient's general health and genetic composition.

Oncologists and Oncologists are medical professionals who specialise in treating cancer, but their roles, training, and approaches to treating cancer differ significantly.

An oncologist is a medical doctor who is qualified in diagnosing and treating cancer through non-surgical methods.

A Oncologist is a qualified medical doctor who uses surgical procedures to treat cancer.
