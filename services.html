Oncology | Cancer Treatment

Surgical oncology is a cancer treatment that uses surgery to remove cancer cells from the body. This can involve cutting out the tumour and any nearby tissues that cancer might affect. The goal of surgical oncology is to remove as much of the cancer as possible while also preserving the patient's overall health and quality of life. This type of treatment can be very complex and requires specialised training. Still, it can be an essential part of cancer care for many people.

Oncologist | Cancer Specialist

A surgical oncologist is a medical professional specialising in treating cancer through surgical procedures. They work with a team of other doctors to develop an individualised treatment plan for each patient.

Surgical oncologists are responsible for removing tumours, comprehending how cancer operates, and staying updated with the latest research in the field. They collaborate with other cancer specialists to ensure that every patient receives the most effective treatment, including chemotherapy, radiation therapy, or other targeted therapies. The primary objective of a surgical oncologist is to aid cancer patients in improving their health, prolonging their life, and achieving the best possible quality of life.
